Clear Your Browser Cache

Browser Cache Issue Detected

Our website has been updated, but your browser might be showing an older version from its cache. To fix this, you need to clear your browser cache or use the "hard refresh" option.

This is a one-time process that will ensure you're seeing the latest version of our website.

Option 1: Hard Refresh (Quickest Method)

Try one of these keyboard shortcuts while on our website:

  • Windows/Linux: Press Ctrl + F5 or Ctrl + Shift + R
  • Mac: Press Command + Shift + R or Shift + click the Refresh button

Option 2: Clear Browser Cache

Google Chrome
  1. Click the three dots in the upper right corner
  2. Select "More tools" > "Clear browsing data"
  3. Select "Cached images and files"
  4. Click "Clear data"
Firefox
  1. Click the menu button (three lines) in the upper right
  2. Select "Settings"
  3. Go to "Privacy & Security"
  4. Scroll to "Cookies and Site Data" and click "Clear Data"
  5. Select "Cached Web Content" and click "Clear"
Safari
  1. Click "Safari" in the menu bar
  2. Select "Preferences"
  3. Go to the "Advanced" tab
  4. Check "Show Develop menu in menu bar"
  5. Close Preferences and click "Develop" in the menu bar
  6. Select "Empty Caches"
Microsoft Edge
  1. Click the three dots in the upper right corner
  2. Select "Settings"
  3. Click "Privacy, search, and services"
  4. Under "Clear browsing data", click "Choose what to clear"
  5. Select "Cached images and files"
  6. Click "Clear now"

Option 3: Use Incognito/Private Browsing

Opening our website in an incognito or private browsing window will load the site without using cached files.
